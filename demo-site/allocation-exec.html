01 · The need

The gap between attendance and connection.

Leaders know their regulars. But without a system, the students who attend occasionally — or start drifting — often slip through with no one noticing until it's too late.

What happens without this

  • Student allocation managed in a spreadsheet — outdated by Wednesday
  • No visibility into who is attending less than they used to
  • Leaders unsure which students are "theirs" for follow-up
  • New students arrive and fall through the gaps
  • Grade leaders each reinventing their own tracking system

What this enables

  • Every active student has a named leader — visible to the whole team
  • At-risk students flagged automatically from attendance data
  • Grade leaders see only their cohort; quad leaders see their bracket
  • Director sees the whole ministry at a glance
  • Import runs weekly — status updates automatically
Connection is the ministry. This makes sure it doesn't slip through admin gaps.
02 · How it works

A weekly rhythm, not a one-off project.

The platform is designed around the natural weekly cycle of youth ministry — import after Sunday, review mid-week, allocate before the following Sunday.

03 · The priority workflow

Active students first. Always.

Not all students need the same urgency of connection. The platform builds the priority decision into the interface rather than leaving it to individual judgement.

⭐ Priority — Active students

Attended 2 or more combined service and/or group events in the last 10. These students have an existing relationship with the ministry and should have a named leader before the week begins. They appear at the top of every picker, search, and list.

Optional — Lower attendance

Attended fewer than 2 events. Visible and allocatable, but presented at lower priority. Includes new students with no attendance history yet. Leaders can still be assigned when capacity allows.

  • The Leaders & Allocation page header shows a live count of active students still pending a leader — the single most important number each week.
  • A warning banner appears until every active student is covered; then a green confirmation replaces it.
  • Each leader card shows a capacity gauge bar — how loaded they are relative to the fair-share target — so uneven allocation is visible at a glance.
  • At-risk students are surfaced prominently in the At Risk tab and can still be allocated even if their attendance is low.

05 · Attendance data

Upload your data the way you already collect it.

The import system is resilient and format-flexible — whether your attendance comes from Planning Center, ChurchSuite, or a spreadsheet, it's designed to just work.

  • Fridays CSV — first_name, last_name, gender, grade, then one date column per Friday session. Attendance values: true/false, yes/no, 1/0.
  • Group / Lifegroup CSV — same format with optional group_name. Lifegroups tracked independently and compared to service attendance.
  • Excel short-date columns — exports from Excel often use "7-Feb" or "14-Mar" instead of "2025-02-07". The system detects and normalises these automatically.
  • Optional enrichment columns — mobile, parent_phone (or guardian_phone), date_of_birth (or birthday). Included if available; not required. Updated on each re-import.
  • Outlier protection — any session where attendance is below 50% of the term average is flagged as an outlier and excluded from trend calculations, preventing anomalous weeks from distorting the picture.
  • Incremental upsert — safe to upload each week; new sessions stack on existing totals; students matched by name, not duplicated.
06 · Attendance trends

Three numbers that drive pastoral action.

The Trends screen gives each role a column chart plus a consistent three-number summary — with previous-term comparison — so trend is visible at a glance without reading a bar.

Fridays — column chart + three-number card

  • Column chart of last 10 sessions with trend badge (↑ ↓ →) — ministry-wide at the top, then role-specific drill-down
  • Three summary numbers below each chart: Unique attenders (pastoral breadth) · Avg students/session (operational load) · Avg sessions/student (individual engagement)
  • Each number shows a "prev term: N" comparison line so movement is immediately visible
  • Director & admin: tap any quad to expand, tap any grade within it — three levels without leaving the page. Dropdowns preserve scroll position.

Lifegroups — same three-number pattern

  • Overview card shows the same three numbers for all enrolled students: avg students/session · unique attenders · avg sessions/student — with prev-term comparison
  • A breadth bar shows what proportion of enrolled students attended at least once (e.g. 8 of 12 enrolled = 67%)
  • Per-grade rows with the same three numbers; tap to expand individual groups (e.g. Friday Group vs Sunday Group) each with their own three-number card and bar
  • Grade logins see their individual groups directly without expanding
  • Export (quad / director / admin) downloads full CSV with per-quad and grade summary
Every leader can see their students. Every director can see the whole picture.
