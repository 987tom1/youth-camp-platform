Start here · everyone

The five things to know first

True for every role, in both seasons.

1 · The tab bar

The buttons along the bottom are how you move around. They change with your role and the season — you only ever see the tabs you need.

2 · The mode badge — pre-camp ↔ at-camp

The badge in the header shows the camp's current season: Pre-camp (amber) or At camp (green). In the live app an admin flips this once and everyone moves over on their next login. In this demo you can tap the badge yourself to preview both.

3 · The Day badge (at camp)

Once at camp, a Day 1 / Day 2 badge controls day-specific screens: Day 1 shows First Day Sign In, Day 2 onward shows Submit Testimonies.

4 · One record per person

Everything — accommodation, payments, blue cards, check-in, notes — reads from a single registration record. No parallel spreadsheets; what you change, everyone sees.

5 · Notices

The Notices tab and your Home screen show the latest announcements. Pre-camp notices stay put; at-camp notices clear themselves a few hours after posting so the list stays current.

Church account

For each church's youth pastor / leader. You see only your own church's people.
Pre-camp

Check your status at a glance

The Home screen opens on a status band for your church.

  • Campers and Leaders registered.
  • Ldr blue card — leaders 18+ still needing a working-with-children check (red until done).
  • Your registration progress bar and days-to-go countdown.

Review your young people

Open My Youth to see everyone you've registered.

  1. Filter by gender and grade to find a group quickly.
  2. Read the Student split and Leader split summaries.
  3. Accommodation shows as Tent or Classroom (exact room names are kept to the camp office).

Share your registration code

Your church's registration code appears on Home — give it to families so their sign-ups land against your church.

Get help

The Help tab answers the common questions (FAQ). Start there before calling the office.

At camp

Daily check-in

  1. Open Check-in. The day picker and gender/grade filters narrow the list.
  2. The count shows how many of the shown group aren't checked in yet.
  3. Tap a name to mark present. There are two check-ins a day.

Sign a youth out (and back in)

When a young person leaves the camp site, record it — this is a safeguarding step, not just admin.

  1. Find them via Search or your youth list and choose Sign out.
  2. Enter the leader's name, the reason, and whether a parent was met. Review the summary, then confirm.
  3. They drop off the daily roll. When they return, Sign back in with your initials.
Every sign-out / sign-in is logged automatically as a record leadership can review.

Write a student note

Capture anything worth passing on (left dinner early, looked unwell). Notes are write-for-church, read-for-leadership — accountable records, not gossip.

Your accommodation

Once the director has allocated rooms, a Your Accommodation tile appears on Home showing where your group is staying.

Zone leader

Oversees one zone (a group of churches). Everything a church can do, plus zone-wide visibility.

See your whole zone

Home and My Youth roll up every church in your zone — campers, leaders, and outstanding blue cards across the zone.

The "By ministry" breakdown

Home shows a per-ministry table — male/female students and leaders, with totals — scoped to your zone, so you can spot a church that's behind.

Send a zone notice

  1. Open Notices and write your announcement.
  2. Set the scope to your zone; post now or schedule it for later.
  3. You can delete a notice you posted.
At camp

Read notes & testimonies across your zone

You can read the student notes and records for every church in your zone — filter by record type (testimony / sign-out·in / student note), ministry and zone; grade and gender show for notes and records.

Export for follow-up

Use the CSV export to take notes and testimonies away for pastoral follow-up — it includes a Category column.

Check-in, search & sign-out

All the on-the-ground tools a church has — Check-in, Search, sign-out/in — work the same for you, across your zone.

Director

Camp-wide oversight. Everything a zone leader can do, across the whole camp, plus budget and accommodation.
Pre-camp

The camp-wide rollup

Home totals every church and zone — campers, leaders, outstanding blue cards — with the by-ministry table for the whole camp.

Budget & costings

Open the Budget & costings card on Home.

  • Fee tiers: tent price, classroom price, half-price voucher, fully sponsored.
  • See totals and break them down per ministry.
  • Camp-wide tent/classroom prices are set in Admin → Camp settings.

Accommodation allocations

Open the Accommodation allocations card.

  1. Classrooms: allocate eligible ministries (those with ≥75% of students in classrooms) by gender. Partial placement fills a room to capacity and leaves the remainder available; multiple ministries can share a room.
  2. Tent City (Male / Female) auto-distributes tent ministries into 7-person tents, keeping leaders in separate tents from students.
  3. Un-allocating cascades sensibly — it back-fills other rooms, then returns the remainder.

Camp-wide notices

You can post notices to the whole camp, a zone, or a church — now or scheduled.

At camp

Everything, camp-wide

Check-in, search, sign-out/in, and the full notes & testimonies log (with CSV export) all work across every zone and church.

Keep the camp informed

Send urgent or normal notices to the whole camp at a moment's notice from Notices.

Admin

Everything a director can do, plus the back-office console that sets the camp up and runs it year to year. There is always exactly one admin.
Set-up & back office

The Admin console

The Admin tab is your control panel — and it's identical in both seasons, so you can prepare at-camp content (schedule, devotionals) while still in pre-camp.

  • Camp settings — name, dates, tent/classroom prices, accommodation lock.
  • Accounts — add church, zone-leader and director logins (the church name must match the registration form).
  • Accommodation — define rooms and their capacity/price.
  • FAQ, Schedule (which items are check-in points), and Devotionals.

Flip the camp into at-camp mode

When camp begins, switch the mode from the Admin console (or the header badge). Every login moves to at-camp on their next sign-in — nothing reinstalled, same accounts and data.

During & after camp

Run the days

You have every director and leader tool, plus live control of schedule, devotionals, FAQ and accounts if something needs changing on the fly.

Reuse it next year

  1. Set defaults — once the camp scaffold (churches, accounts, accommodation, FAQ, schedule, devotionals) is right, snapshot it as next year's baseline.
  2. After camp, Start new year — purges registrants, campers, notes and notifications, then restores the baseline. The admin account and camp settings are kept.
Set it up once; every following year starts from a clean, familiar scaffold.
