Training & How-To

A practical guide to using the Youth Allocation Platform. Pick your role and follow the steps that match what you see. Keep this open on your phone during your first session.

Demo only · fictional students · no real personal information
Signing in. Open the app and tap a Quick Login button — Admin, Director, Grade login, or Quad — or type the email directly. All demo passwords are demo1234. The role badge in the top-right corner shows your current scope. Hover or long-press it to see a description of what you can access.
Start here · everyone

Four things to know first

These apply to every role before you dive into your specific tasks.

1 · The bottom navigation bar

The four icons along the bottom are your most-used sections for your role. The layout changes with your role:

  • Grade login: Home | My Students | Trends | At Risk
  • Quad login: Home | Leaders & Alloc | Trends | At Risk
  • Director: Home | Leaders & Alloc | Trends | At Risk
  • Admin: Home | Leaders & Alloc | Trends | At Risk

Additional features appear as Quick Action tiles on the Home screen and in the desktop nav bar. What's available depends on your role — grade logins get Student Search; director and admin also get Import.

2 · Active vs optional students

Students are split into two tiers throughout the app:

⭐ Active — allocate first

Attended 2 or more combined events (services + groups) in the last 10. These students have an existing relationship and should be connected to a leader as a priority.

Optional — when ready

Attended fewer than 2 events. Still visible and allocatable, but shown at lower priority. Includes students with no attendance data yet.

The goal is to ensure every active student has a leader before moving to the optional pool.

3 · The Phone View toggle

The small button in the corner (📱 Phone View) wraps the app in a realistic phone frame — useful for training and demonstrations on a desktop. Click again to switch back to the full desktop layout.

4 · Nothing is permanent in the demo

All changes (allocations, leader edits, imports) stay in your browser session. Refreshing the page resets everything. In the live app, data persists to the server and is visible to other logins in real time.

Grade Login

One login per year group per gender — e.g. Grade 9 Girls (grade9f@) and Grade 9 Boys (grade9m@) are separate accounts. Each login sees only their own gender cohort's students and leaders.

Your home screen at a glance

Your Home screen opens with an attendance hero card showing this term's picture for your cohort as a compact table — Youth (Fridays) and Groups (Lifegroups) rows, with Unique and Avg/wk columns. A Previous term comparison appears below in the same format.

Below the hero card: Quick Actions (Leaders & Allocation, Student Search), then a compact Allocation Summary strip — Total / Allocated / Pending / At Risk — for your cohort at a glance.

Start here each session. If Pending is above zero and there are active students without a leader, that's your immediate task.
Step 1 · Set up your leaders

Adding a leader

  1. Tap Leaders & Alloc from Quick Actions on Home (or the desktop nav).
  2. Tap + Add Leader (top-right).
  3. Enter their full name. Select a gender (female/male) — this controls which students appear in their picker.
  4. Your grade is auto-applied; you don't need to set it manually.
  5. Tap Add Leader. They appear as a card immediately.
Add all your leaders before starting allocation — it makes the picker workflow much faster.

Editing or removing a leader

  1. On any leader card, tap the ✏️ pencil icon to edit name, gender, or active status.
  2. Tap the 🗑 trash icon to remove a leader — this also removes all their student assignments.
Removing a leader cannot be undone via undo toast — their allocations are also cleared. Use the Active toggle instead if you just want to temporarily hide them.
Step 2 · Allocate active students

The allocation workflow

Tap Leaders & Alloc from Quick Actions. Each leader in your grade appears as a card showing:

  • A capacity fill-bar — how loaded they are relative to the target (total gender-matching students ÷ leaders in scope).
  • Student count vs target, plus an at-risk flag if any of their students need attention.
  • The first 8 assigned students with attendance % and risk status.
  • A + Add Students button that opens the search picker.
The fill-bar turns amber when under-loaded and red at 90%+ of target. Aim for roughly equal loading across leaders of the same gender.

Using the student picker

  1. Tap + Add Students on a leader card.
  2. By default the picker shows students who match the leader's gender and grade, split into:
    — ⭐ Active students at the top (≥2 attendances) — allocate these first.
    — Other students below — fewer than 2 attendances, lower priority.
  3. Use the dropdown filter to switch modes:
    — Grade match (default) — leader's own grade and gender.
    — Fewer than 20% attendance — low-attendance students, for targeted pastoral follow-up.
    — Unallocated from other grades — same-gender students from other years not yet assigned to anyone.
  4. The search bar finds any same-gender student ministry-wide, regardless of grade or allocation status.
  5. Tap + to assign. Tap Done when finished.
Cross-grade allocation is supported — assigning a student from another year is fine as long as their gender matches the leader's profile.

Removing a student from a leader

On the leader card, tap the × next to any student's name. An Undo toast appears for 5 seconds — tap it immediately if you removed someone by mistake.

Step 3 · Review at-risk students

Checking the At Risk list

  1. Tap At Risk in the bottom nav.
  2. Students are grouped by severity: Stopped Attending (most urgent), At Risk, Declining, Watch List.
  3. Each card shows Fridays and Lifegroup attendance with a trend arrow (↑ ↓ →) comparing to last term. A student at 30% this term who was 75% last term shows a red ↓ immediately.
  4. Tap ✏️ to manually update status if you have context the data doesn't capture (holiday, illness).
At-risk students need pastoral connection most. Allocate them via the student picker — they appear in the "Other students" section but can be assigned to any leader.
Step 4 · Check attendance trends

Viewing Trends — Fridays

Tap Trends in the bottom nav. The Fridays tab shows a ministry-wide column chart, then a Grade details card with a column chart for your cohort and three summary numbers:

  • Unique attenders — how many distinct students came at least once
  • Avg students/session — how busy each Friday is operationally
  • Avg sessions/student — how consistently individuals are engaging

A "prev term: N" line under each number shows how it compares to last term.

Viewing Trends — Lifegroups

Tap the Lifegroups tab. You'll see an overview card, then individual lifegroup cards for your grade — one per group (Friday Group and Sunday Group). Each shows the same three numbers plus a breadth bar showing what proportion of enrolled students attended at all:

  • Avg students/session — how many typically show up each week
  • Unique attenders — how many distinct students came at least once
  • Avg sessions/student — how consistently each enrolled student attends
If one group has significantly lower unique attenders than the other, that's worth a pastoral conversation — it may indicate a scheduling conflict or group dynamic issue.
Step 5 · Leader self-view (My Students)

Using My Students to look up student contacts

Tap My Students in the bottom nav. Select a leader from the dropdown. You'll see their allocated students with:

  • Mobile number (tap to dial directly on phone)
  • Birthday and parent/guardian phone number
  • Attendance % for Fridays and lifegroup
  • Coloured attendance pips for recent Fridays — green = attended, grey = absent
Share this screen with leaders before a follow-up day so they can reach their students directly. No separate contact list needed.
Step 6 · Export when done

Exporting allocations

On the Leaders & Alloc page, tap ⬇ Export (top right). A CSV downloads with one row per allocation: leader, grades, gender, student, grade, gender, Fridays%, at-risk status.

Quad Login

One login per quad (e.g. Girls Yr 7–9, g79@). You have a read-only view of all leaders and students in your gender + age bracket. Grade logins make allocation changes; you oversee and monitor.

Your home screen

Home opens with a compact attendance hero card — Youth (Fridays) and Groups rows showing Unique and Avg/wk for this term, with a previous-term comparison below. Tap Grade detail to expand per-grade attendance figures broken into Youth and Groups columns.

Below that: Quick Actions (Student Search, My Students) then a grade-by-grade allocation progress bar showing how allocated each year is.

Leaders & Allocation — your main view

Tap Leaders & Alloc in the bottom nav. You'll see every active leader in your quad as an allocation card, showing:

  • Their capacity fill-bar — how many students they have vs the target for their gender group.
  • Assigned student count, at-risk count, and a status indicator (all clear / declining).
  • The first 8 assigned students with attendance % and risk chips.

Use the Year filter chips at the top to narrow to a specific grade in your bracket (e.g. show only Year 8 leaders). As a quad login you can view but not add or reassign students.

This is your primary weekly check — scan for leaders with many at-risk students and follow up with the relevant grade login.

At Risk — your quad

Tap At Risk in the bottom nav. Students in your quad are grouped by severity — Stopped, At Risk, Declining, Watch. Each card shows current and previous-term attendance with trend arrows so you can see whether a decline is new or ongoing. Use this to brief grade logins on who needs priority attention.

Year filter chips narrow to a specific grade — useful when briefing an individual grade login.

Trends — Fridays

Tap Trends in the bottom nav. The Fridays tab shows a ministry-wide column chart, then a card for your quad with three summary numbers — Unique attenders · Avg students/session · Avg sessions/student — each with a prev-term comparison. Per-grade rows below show the same numbers inline.

Trends — Lifegroups

Tap the Lifegroups tab. An overview card shows the three numbers for all enrolled students plus a breadth bar (what proportion attended at all). Per-grade rows below — tap any to expand and see individual group cards (e.g. Friday Group vs Sunday Group), each with their own three-number card and breadth bar.

Dropdowns expand in place — your scroll position is preserved so you don't jump back to the top of the page.

Student Search

Tap Student Search from Quick Actions to look up any individual in your quad by name. Year filter chips narrow to a specific grade. Tap any student to see their full detail — attendance, leader assignment, and contact info.

Why you can't make allocation changes

Quad logins are read-only for allocations. Allocation authority stays with each grade login. Contact the relevant grade login (e.g. grade9f@ for Grade 9 Girls) if a change is needed.

Director

Ministry-wide access. You can do everything a grade or quad login can, across all years and genders — plus importing attendance data.

Your home screen overview

Home opens with an attendance hero card for the whole ministry — a compact table showing Youth (Fridays) and Groups rows, with Unique and Avg/wk columns. Previous-term comparison appears below in the same format. Tap Grade detail to expand per-grade figures with separate Youth and Groups columns.

Below that: Quick Actions (Student Search, My Students, Import), then By Quad allocation cards and a grade-by-grade progress bar. A "Last Import" card appears if data has been uploaded this term.

Your bottom nav is Home | Leaders & Alloc | Trends | At Risk. Leaders & Allocation is always one tap away in the nav — no need to go via Quick Actions.
Importing attendance data

What the import does

The Import page accepts two types of files:

Fridays CSV

Required: first_name, last_name, gender, grade. Date columns = attended. Optional: mobile/phone, parent_phone/guardian_phone, date_of_birth/birthday.

Group / Lifegroup CSV

Same format. Optional: group_name. All optional contact columns also accepted.

Each upload is incremental — new sessions stack on existing totals. Students matched by full name; new students added automatically. Contact details are updated each time they appear.

Date columns can be ISO (2025-02-07) or Excel short-dates (7-Feb, 14-Feb). The system normalises them automatically — no reformatting needed.
Sessions below 50% of the term average are flagged as outliers and excluded from trend calculations, preventing a low-turnout week (e.g. school camp) from distorting the picture.

Running an import

  1. Tap Import from Quick Actions on Home.
  2. Select the tab — Fridays or Groups.
  3. Drag a CSV file onto the upload area, or tap to browse.
  4. A preview shows type detected, date columns found, optional fields, and a sample row — check it looks right.
  5. Tap Import into Demo to commit.
  6. A success message shows students updated and sessions added.
The demo simulates the import. In the live app, the full file is processed, totals updated, and at-risk status recomputed for all students in the import.
Managing allocation across the ministry

Leaders & Allocation — full ministry view

Tap Leaders & Alloc in the bottom nav. You see every active leader ministry-wide as allocation cards — the same view grade logins use, but across all grades and genders. Use the Year and Gender filter chips at the top to narrow to any cohort (e.g. Year 10 Female only). You can add leaders, assign students, and export from this single view.

The status banner at the top shows how many active students are still pending a leader — the single most important number each week.

Attendance Trends — Fridays

Tap Trends then the Fridays tab. The ministry-wide column chart appears first, then collapsible quad cards. Tap any quad to expand its chart and three-number summary — Unique attenders · Avg students/session · Avg sessions/student with prev-term comparison — then tap any grade row within it for that grade's own chart and numbers. Dropdowns expand in place, preserving your scroll position.

Attendance Trends — Lifegroups

Tap the Lifegroups tab. A ministry overview card shows the same three numbers plus a breadth bar (proportion of enrolled students who attended at all). Per-grade rows below — tap any to expand and see individual group cards (Friday Group / Sunday Group), each with their own three-number card and breadth bar, plus prev-term comparison. Tap ⬇ Export for a full CSV.

Recomputing at-risk status

After importing new data, tap At Risk then 🔄 Recompute. This recalculates every student's status against the current thresholds. At-risk cards show previous-term attendance alongside current with trend arrows — a decline is visible in context immediately.

Exporting allocations

On Leaders & Alloc, tap ⬇ Export for a CSV of all allocations ministry-wide — leader, grades, gender, student, grade, gender, Fridays%, at-risk status.

Admin

Everything a director can do, plus the back-office console for accounts, settings, allocation lock, and year-rollover. There is always exactly one admin account.

Your home screen and nav

Identical to the director view — Home | Leaders & Alloc | Trends | At Risk in the bottom nav, with Student Search, My Students, Import, and Admin console in Quick Actions. The Admin console is the only area unique to this login.

The Admin console

Tap Admin from Quick Actions. Four tabs:

  • Settings — ministry name, service/group labels, allocation lock date, and at-risk thresholds.
  • Accounts — add/edit/deactivate all logins, grouped by role (Admin, Director, Quad Leaders, Grade Logins).
  • Data — save defaults, start a new year, or run a full reset.
  • Audit — a log of all admin actions (settings changes, resets) with timestamp and who performed them.
Setting up a new term

Creating user accounts

  1. Go to Admin → Accounts, tap + Add Account.
  2. Enter a display name. The email field auto-suggests a convention (e.g. grade9f@youth.ministry for Grade 9 Girls, g79@youth.ministry for Girls 7–9 quad) when you select the role and year/quad — edit if needed.
  3. Choose the role: Grade (select year), Quad (select which quad), Director, or Admin.
  4. Tap Create Account. Share the email and a temporary password with the login holder.
Accounts are listed in groups — Grade Logins together, Quad Leaders together — so it's easy to confirm all 24 grade logins (12 years × 2 genders) exist before the term starts.

Configuring at-risk thresholds

Go to Admin → Settings → At-Risk Thresholds. Two rates define the three status bands:

  • Regular Rate — students at or above this rate (default 3/4 = 75%) are "regular".
  • At-Risk Rate — students below this rate (default 1/2 = 50%) are "at risk". Between the two = "declining".

After changing thresholds, use Recompute on the At Risk page to reapply them to all students.

End-of-term and year rollover

Saving defaults before rollover

Go to Admin → Data → Save Defaults. This snapshots the current account list and leader profiles as the baseline for next term. Run this once the setup is right — before clearing any data.

Starting a new term / year

  1. First, Save Defaults (see above).
  2. Tap Start New Year. This snapshots each student's current attendance into their "previous term" fields (used for trend comparison on the At Risk screen), then zeros current-term stats and clears allocations. Students are retained so next term's import can update them by name match.
  3. Import the new term's attendance CSV to repopulate attendance totals.
  4. Grade logins then reallocate students to leaders for the new term.
Because students are retained, the At Risk page shows previous-term attendance alongside the new term from the first import — making early declines visible immediately.
Start New Year cannot be undone. Save Defaults first and confirm with your team.

Full Reset (use with caution)

Admin → Data → Full Reset clears everything including leaders and students. Use only to start completely fresh — e.g. setting up the platform for a completely different ministry.

Reading the audit log

Every admin action — settings changes, lock date updates, resets — is recorded in Admin → Audit with a timestamp and the account that performed it.
